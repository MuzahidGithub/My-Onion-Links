Strategy vs Tactics

Aspect Strategy Tactics
Definition Strategy is the intent Tactics is putting intent to action
Purpose Determines what needs to be done and why Determines how it must be done
Nature Involves intentional and focused high-level thinking that defines the direction to take Involves concrete actions and steps to implementation in line with the direction
Alignment Aligned with the goals, objectives Aligned with the strategy
Execution Requires focus on defining the future Requires day-to-day execution
Changeability It is difficult to change, though entirely possible It is very easy to change
Perspective Needs outward perspective Needs inward view
Responsibility Typically formed by leaders within the organization Typically defined and executed by managers
Focus Primary focus is on effectiveness, doing the right thing Primary focus is on efficiency, doing it right with less resources, time, and money
Evaluation Difficult to measure and evaluate Easy to evaluate through well-defined metrics
Planning Strategic planning does not include execution details Tactical plans include timelines and implementation
Intangibility Strategy is intangible Tactics are tangible
